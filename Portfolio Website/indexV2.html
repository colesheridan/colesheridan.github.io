About & Contact

I'm Cole Sheridan, a University of Utah Electrical Engineering graduate focused on power systems, distribution, grid infrastructure, and controls. My background combines engineering analysis with hands-on troubleshooting in real operating environments.

In school, I worked on transformer modeling, open- and short-circuit testing, voltage regulation, efficiency, three-phase machines, MATLAB studies, and PowerWorld-style power-system analysis. Outside the classroom, I have worked directly with electrical distribution, motors, sensors, controls, and electromechanical equipment in a high-uptime facility.

Professionally, I bring experience from SCADAPack PLC/RTU water infrastructure work, customer-facing technical support, and day-to-day equipment diagnostics. I'm currently seeking entry-level electrical engineering roles in power systems, utilities, transmission/distribution planning, grid infrastructure, industrial controls, or related hardware-focused engineering.
